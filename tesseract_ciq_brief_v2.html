The Problem This Solves for Your Customers
Reproducibility is a hard requirement
In scientific computing, results that change on identical inputs aren't results — they're noise. RAG and vector search are non-deterministic by design. Tesseract's retrieval navigates by geometric address. Same query, same result, every time. That's a structural property of the architecture, not a configuration option.
Airgapped means airgapped
Every major AI retrieval product — Pinecone, Weaviate, LangChain — calls home. Tesseract has zero external dependencies by architecture. Not by configuration. Not by an offline mode bolted on later. It was built to run inside the fence from day one. It deploys the same way Fuzzball does.
The system knows what it doesn't know
Current AI systems hallucinate confident wrong answers. In regulated, defense, or safety-critical environments that's not a quality problem — it's a liability problem. Tesseract enforces rejection at the architecture level. If the answer isn't in the corpus, the system says so. Every claim traces to a specific source record.
Full auditability by design
Every retrieval decision is replayable. Every generated claim cites its source record. Every query produces the same address in semantic space regardless of when or where it runs. For national labs, defense contractors, and regulated research environments — this is the difference between deployable and not.
